Velký Újezd

Velký Újezd is a market town in Olomouc District in the Olomouc Region of the Czech Republic. It has about 1,400 inhabitants.





== Geography ==

Velký Újezd is located about 15 kilometres (9 mi) east of Olomouc. It lies in the Nízký Jeseník range. The highest point is at 479 m (1,572 ft) above sea level.





== History ==

The first written mention of Velký Újezd is 1301. In 1381, Velký Újezd is referred to as a market town for the first time. From the end of the 14th century until the 16th century, it belonged to the Helfštýn estate, then it was annexed to the Veselíčko estate. During the Thirty Years' War, Velký Újezd was badly damaged and ceased to be a market town. The next period of prosperity came during the rule of the Podstatský family in the 18th century.
