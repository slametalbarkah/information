Shalom Kadosh

Shalom Kadosh (Hebrew: שלום קדוש; born 1947 or 1948) is an Israeli chef. He has been considered the unofficial chef of Israel's prime ministers, including Menachem Begin, Yitzhak Rabin, Ariel Sharon, and Benjamin Netanyahu. French food critic Gilles Pudlowski has dubbed Kadosh the "Kosher Bocuse".





== Early and personal life ==

Kadosh was born in Morocco, near Marrakesh, into a family of nine siblings. The family immigrated to Israel when he was 14, settling in Afula. He became interested in traveling as a young man, and began studying at a cooking school in Haifa so he could save up for travel funds.

Kadosh and his wife, Zohara, have a daughter together.

In late March 2021, Kadosh sustained fractures to his neck and skull during an attempted robbery at a gas station in Jerusalem, several hours before his daughter's wedding. He was brought to the Hadassah Ein Kerem Medical Center in serious condition, and was put into a medically induced coma. Kadosh regained consciousness three weeks later.
